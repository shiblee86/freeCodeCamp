Kazi Nazrul Islam

The Rebel Poet

 
 
 

Kazi Narul Islam, also known as Nazrul was a Bengali poet, writer, musician, and revolutionary. Through his poems he inspired the people of India to seek independce from British rule.

Nazrul's life:

Year Event
1899 Is born on Friday 26 May 1899 in Paschim Bardhaman district of West Bengal
1905 Starts his education to become an imam
1908 Nazrul's father, Kazi Faqeer Ahmed passes away
1910 Begins his literature studies
1917 Joins the British Indian Army
1919 Nazrul's poems; "Life of a Vagabond" and "Freedom" published
1922 Earns the nick "Rebel Poet" and is arrested by the British police
1931 Is released from prison
1941 Falls ill to dimentia
1972 Becomes the national poet of Bangladesh
1976 Dies from his long illness
